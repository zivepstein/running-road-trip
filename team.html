Nico Ericksen-Deriso

After watching his Grandfather detiorate from an everyday runner to an eventual Alzheimer's patient, Nico has taken his Grandfather's place, running to further advance the battle against one of the world's most difficult diseases.

Sadie Witt

When I first began running, it was for the purpose of clearing my head after a long day. It gradually took on more meaning. It became a social outlet; when I’m apart of a team or club, I’m apart of a strong community. The day of my first high school cross country meet, my grandfather passed away. Having always been my personal cheerleader as well as an active person himself, I decided to race and that I would race not for myself, but for him. As I’ve learned, running holds a greater significance that is beyond just for oneself. Through the RRT, I will be running for Alzheimer’s.

Bailee Mulholland

“It doesn't matter whether you're the lion or the gazelle-when the sun comes up, you'd better be running.”
-Christopher McDougall

Running was a transformative experience for me. It helped me to understand the beauty of early mornings and the focus and hard work it takes in order to achieve a goal. I've met my best friends through running. By embarking on this road trip, and raising awareness and funds for Alzheimer's , I hope to spread the sense of community and support that I've recieved by running.

Ziv Epstein

Running is more than a sport. It's a way of life that brings communities together. Growing up in Boulder, Colordo, I've seen this first hand. Now I want to bring this sense of community and empowerment that running can provide to a larger community at scale. I can't think of a better cause for this project to support. I will providing the technical resources to get RRT moving.
