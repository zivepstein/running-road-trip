Our Mission

ADVENTURE / CONNECTION / PASSION / PHILANTHROPY / FRIENDSHIP

~48 States, 48 Runs~

→ The Running Road Trip project is an adventure around the continental United States that will be embarked on by four average runners with a passion of running. For roughly two months, the road trip will weave in and out of each state, where we will complete an interesting run. Throughout this endeavor, we will be raising money and awareness for the detrimental disease of Alzheimer’s. Keep up with our progress through Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter!

Our team

Nico Ericksen-Deriso

After watching his Grandfather detiorate from an everyday runner to an eventual Alzheimer's patient, Nico has taken his Grandfather's place, running to further advance the battle against one of the world's most difficult diseases.

Sadie Witt

When I first began running, it was for the purpose of clearing my head after a long day. It gradually took on more meaning. It became a social outlet; when I’m apart of a team or club, I’m apart of a strong community. The day of my first high school cross country meet, my grandfather passed away. Having always been my personal cheerleader as well as an active person himself, I decided to race and that I would race not for myself, but for him. As I’ve learned, running holds a greater significance that is beyond just for oneself. Through the RRT, I will be running for Alzheimer’s.

Bailee Mulholland

To me, this quote is the epitome of what it means to be a runner. Some people are faster than you without training, and some can go farther. But in the end, none of that matters. The only thing that really matters is whether you got up and you trained and you put your heart and soul into doing your best. If you've done that, no matter how your races go, you can be proud of yourself.
